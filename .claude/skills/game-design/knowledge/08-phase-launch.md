# Phase 7: LAUNCH — Knowledge Reference

By launch day it's already too late to build an audience. Marketing and timing must start early.

---

## The Golden Rule of Mobile Launch

> ~50 games ship on Steam daily and anything not caught in the new-releases window effectively
> disappears. The same pattern applies to app stores with even higher daily volume.
> By launch day it's already too late to build an audience.

**Start marketing at first prototype, not at launch.**

---

## Pre-Launch Marketing Checklist (Start at PROTOTYPE phase)

- [ ] Social media presence active (TikTok preferred for discoverability, Instagram for visual)
- [ ] Devlog / building-in-public cadence started
- [ ] Store page live (even with placeholder art) to capture early interest
- [ ] Email/Discord waitlist capturing interested players
- [ ] App preview video shot on real device (not simulator)
- [ ] Influencer / content creator outreach started 8+ weeks before launch

Building in public from day one attracts a community around the **process**, not just the product.
This audience is more loyal and harder to build post-launch.

---

## App Store Optimization (ASO) — Final Checklist

Priority order (by conversion impact):

1. **Icon**
   - Test 3+ variants via A/B (App Store Connect allows this)
   - Must communicate core loop, not genre label
   - Readable at 60×60 pixels (the search result size)
   - No text in icon

2. **Video Preview (iOS) / Feature Graphic + Video (Android)**
   - Autoplay on iOS → first 3 seconds must show core gameplay loop
   - No cinematics; no title cards; gameplay first
   - Portrait for casual; landscape for mid-core
   - Target 15–30 seconds; cover why it's fun, not what it is

3. **Screenshots**
   - First 3 visible without scrolling → must sell the game alone
   - Screenshot 1: core mechanic in action
   - Screenshot 2: progression / reward moment
   - Screenshot 3: depth / content breadth
   - Add short captions (2–4 words) on screenshots

4. **Title + Subtitle (iOS) / Short Description (Android)**
   - Title: game name + 1–2 high-intent keywords
   - Subtitle: benefit statement, not genre label
   - Example: "Merge Town: Build & Relax" not "Merge Town: City Builder"

5. **Ratings strategy**
   - Prompt after 3 sessions AND a win (never after loss, never on session 1)
   - Respond to every negative review within 48 hours
   - 4.0+ stars required for algorithm visibility

## Soft Launch → Global Strategy

**Never global launch without soft launch data.**

Soft launch gates:
- D1 retention ≥ 35% (below this: core loop problem, fix before UA spend)
- D7 retention ≥ 18%
- Crash-free rate ≥ 99%
- Core monetization funnel working (first IAP / first ad view)

Soft launch markets: Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Nordics (similar to US/EU, lower CPIs)

After soft launch:
- Iterate on D1 first (biggest LTV multiplier)
- Then D7, then D30
- Add languages before global: EN, DE, FR, ES, PT, JA, KO, ZH cover 80% of revenue

## Launch Timing Strategy

**Schedule around algorithmic windows**:
- iOS: App of the Day / Games featuring = huge install spike; requires press pitch 6–8 weeks ahead
- Google Play: Editor's Choice / New + Updated = algorithm-driven but editorial-influenced
- New Year / back to school (September) / holiday season (November–December) = high download volume
  but also highest competition

**Avoid**: launching same week as a major competitor in your genre.

**Coordinate**: launch update/1.0 milestone with platform launch buzz when possible.

## User Acquisition (UA) Launch Plan

Sequence:
1. **Launch day burst**: concentrate UA spend in first 48 hours for new release ranking boost
2. **Creative testing**: launch with 5+ ad creatives; kill underperformers in 72 hours
3. **Winning creative identification**: 1 creative will outperform; scale that one
4. **Payback modeling**: if CPI × LTV ratio > 1 at D30 → scale; if not → pause and fix retention

UA creative rules:
- Gameplay-first: show the satisfying micro-loop in first 3 seconds
- Authentic gameplay: fake gameplay ads have higher CTR but lower retention (mislead → churn)
- UGC-style ads: user-filmed, lo-fi → often outperform produced ads for casual
- Fear-of-missing-out hooks: "95% of players can't beat level 5"

## Community & Retention Marketing

**Push notifications** (re-engagement):
- D1: "Your progress is waiting" (reward available)
- D3 lapsed: specific open loop ("You were 3 moves from winning...")
- D7 lapsed: social proof ("10,000 players joined this week")
- Frequency: max 1/day; more = uninstall trigger

**Live service / update cadence** (if applicable):
- Run two-speed: patch bugs fast (signal responsiveness); take longer on major content
- Move things up the schedule when players are loudly excited about them
- Plan 1.0 milestone as a capstone + coincide with new platform launches

## Pricing Psychology

- **Free with ads + optional IAP**: highest download volume, lower revenue per user
- **$0.99**: psychological barrier exists; often earns less than F2P equivalent
- **$2.99–$4.99**: "premium quality" signal; viable for strong-IP or high-review-count games
- **Starter pack**: limited-time (first 48 hours after install) is highest-converting IAP
- **Sale windows**: 30-day price-increase cooldown before discount; plan raises + sales in advance

---

## YouTube/Social as Owned Audience

YouTube ad revenue is negligible for small channels — the value is **owned pipeline**:
- YouTube channel = persistent free ad pipeline for your store page
- More durable than any single press hit
- Building in public pre-launch creates an audience around the process
